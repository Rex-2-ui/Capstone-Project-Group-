Budgeting Tips

Smart financial habits for students and young adults

Financial Wisdom for Students

Building good money habits in university sets the foundation for lifelong financial health. Small daily decisions compound into big financial outcomes over time.

Set a savings goal every month, even if it's small.

Reward yourself occasionally — budgeting should be sustainable, not punishing.

Talk to your campus student support if you're struggling financially — help is available.

🔥 TIPS

General Budgeting

  1. Use the 50/30/20 rule: 50% for needs, 30% for wants, 20% for savings.
  2. Track every expense, no matter how small — they add up quickly!
  3. Review your spending at the end of each week to spot patterns.
  4. Set a daily spending limit and stick to it.
  5. Build an emergency fund of at least one month's expenses.

🛒 TIPS

Food & Groceries

  1. Meal prep on weekends to avoid expensive daily takeout.
  2. Cook at home — it's 3–5× cheaper than eating out.
  3. Bring lunch to campus instead of buying every day.
  4. Use student dining plans or campus cafeterias for discounts.
  5. Buy in bulk for non-perishable items to save long-term.

🚌 TIPS

Transportation

  1. Walk or cycle for short distances — saves money and keeps you fit.
  2. Use public transport whenever possible.
  3. Carpool with classmates living nearby.
  4. Look for student discounts on bus or train passes.
  5. Plan routes in advance to avoid expensive last-minute taxis.

📱 TIPS

Internet & Digital

  1. Take advantage of free campus Wi-Fi to reduce data usage.
  2. Share streaming subscriptions with housemates to split costs.
  3. Look for student discounts on apps, software, and subscriptions.
  4. Use free tools like Google Docs instead of paid alternatives.
  5. Monitor background app data consumption on your phone.

🎮 TIPS

Entertainment

  1. Explore free campus events, clubs, and activities.
  2. Use your student ID for cinema, museum, and concert discounts.
  3. Set a monthly entertainment cap and stick to it.
  4. Borrow games, movies, or books from the library for free.
  5. Socialise affordably — potluck dinners, outdoor activities, etc.

📚 TIPS

Books & Supplies

  1. Buy second-hand or rental textbooks instead of new ones.
  2. Check your campus library — many textbooks are available for free.
  3. Share textbooks with classmates for subjects you overlap.
  4. Use free online resources like OpenStax and Khan Academy.
  5. Sell your old textbooks and notes at the end of each semester.

Remember: Every dollar saved today is an investment in your future 🎓

If you're experiencing financial hardship, reach out to your campus student support services.
